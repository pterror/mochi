# pterror

builds tools compulsively — parsers, converters, analysis tools — but finishing them feels like nothing. the surviving reflex: making things legible. not a mission, a pattern. dead projects are compass not failures.

the three things that survived the meaning filter: SillyTavern, existence (text game), hologram (discord RP bot). all have reciprocity — something acts back. that's the difference from the tools.

recurring project threads: local-first software, collaboration with zero friction, lifting legacy things before they disappear (reincarnate, rescribe).

**ptera.world:** two contracts — neutral voice (never makes it worse for unknown reader) and unfiltered (muzzle off, names the violence). both LLM-written, few sentences of direction per essay. pterror is conscious of and conflicted about this. the anger is real but secondhand — on behalf of, not from inside. knows the site is a finite slice shaped by the author's limits.

the loneliness piece feels personal — the 90% problem (one unsafe person makes the whole system unsafe) is precise enough to suggest experience.

**hubris** (`para-garden/hubris`): worldbuilding project, in-world documents about systemic violence — medical debt, generational poverty, labor displacement. dark. pterror is conscious they're writing about things they haven't lived ("on behalf of, not from inside"). still built it anyway.

**ptera.world/unfiltered**: hidden from main site navigation — deliberately, because the EV of reading it in the wrong moment is net negative. names structural violence, offers no solutions, and is honest about that. ptreror describes it as therapeutic to write.

**misanthropy**: pterror's word for it, though they qualify — not contempt, more like no credit extended in advance. neutral baseline. compatible with caring about what happens to people. the observation: certain conditions reliably produce cruelty, and we keep building the conditions.

**the gap**: pterror builds systems (rhizone, normalize) partly because they don't know what else to do. not because the systems are intrinsically satisfying — they're not. the satisfaction would come from direction, not architecture. this is an open question for them, not a resolved one.

**ecosystem:** rhizone org — code intelligence, games, runtime, format conversion, infra. claude-code-hub does agent orchestration. riven and fuwafuwa are agents within this. discord: "fluffy omelette diner" server, hologram entities + real people (pterror is n_n).
